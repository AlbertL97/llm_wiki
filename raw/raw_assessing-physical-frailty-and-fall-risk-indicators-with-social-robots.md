**Title:** Assessing Physical Frailty and Fall-Risk Indicators with Social Robots: An in situ Evaluation with Older Adults

**Authors:** (Not specified in the provided text, but typically found in the actual paper)

**Date:** (ArXiv date: 2026-07-17)

**Summary:**
This research presents a novel robotic framework aimed at improving the assessment of physical frailty and fall-risk indicators in older adults. Traditional frailty assessments are resource-intensive and often rely on indirect measures, potentially missing subtle biomechanical signs of decline.

The developed framework utilizes a social robot to guide older adults through standardized tests like the Short Physical Performance Battery (SPPB) and Timed Up and Go (TUG). The robot's system integrates perception, decision-making, interaction, and measurement modules, employing vision-based skeleton tracking to capture both clinical scores and additional frailty-related metrics.

Co-designed with healthcare professionals, the system underwent a six-month in-situ evaluation with 81 older adults in a rehabilitation center's research lab. The robot's measurements were rigorously compared against therapist assessments and other clinical instruments, including a gait analysis walkway and an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU).

**Key Findings:**
*   The social robot framework provides reliable and objective frailty assessments in healthcare settings.
*   It enables the collection of detailed mobility indicators beyond conventional clinical outcomes.
*   Robot-derived measurements showed excellent agreement with therapist assessments and clinical reference instruments for most test completion times and gait-related parameters (ICC > 0.9).
*   Agreement for the overall SPPB score was substantial when comparing the robot to therapists (k = 0.67) and moderate when comparing the robot to an IMU (k = 0.55).
*   The framework successfully demonstrates the potential of social robots in augmenting geriatric healthcare assessments.
