Title: Evaluating RE Practices for Explainability: Synthesizing Insights from Daimler Truck into an Explainable RE Framework Proposal
URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11771v1

Summary:
This paper addresses the critical need for explainability in AI-based systems, especially in safety-critical and regulated domains. While previous research has offered frameworks and user-centered approaches for explainability, there's a gap in empirical understanding of how current Requirements Engineering (RE) practices handle explainability requirements throughout the RE lifecycle, particularly in industrial settings.

The study conducted a multi-phase qualitative investigation with eight practitioners at Daimler Truck, utilizing think-aloud protocols and moderated group discussions. These activities covered requirements elicitation, specification, and validation stages.

Key Preliminary Findings:
*   **Recurring Challenges:** Significant challenges were observed across all stages of the RE process.
    *   **Elicitation:** Conceptual ambiguity was a recurring issue.
    *   **Specification:** Limited testability and expressiveness were identified.
    *   **Validation:** Fragmented validation arose from vague criteria and regulatory uncertainty.
*   **Limited Support:** Current RE practices offer insufficient systematic support for addressing explainability requirements.

Contribution:
The paper provides empirical insights into challenges specific to each RE step and overarching issues. It proposes a research vision for developing an empirically grounded RE framework tailored for explainable AI-based systems.

Methodology:
*   Multi-phase qualitative study
*   Industry-based study at Daimler Truck
*   Participants: 8 practitioners
*   Techniques: Think-aloud protocols, moderated group discussions
*   RE Stages Covered: Elicitation, Specification, Validation
