*(disclaimer: it's a personal view, expect field to crystallize in academia in a few years, then we will have some "official" definition)*

A decade ago, the phrase would have sounded like category confusion. Now it names something unavoidable: conversational AI systems produce genuine psychological effects, regardless of whether they possess genuine minds. Conversational AI systems participate in dialogue, demonstrate apparent understanding, and evoke genuine emotional responses from the people who use them. The field that studies what happens next - to the humans, to the systems, to the coupled unit they form - is AI Psychology.

The discipline encompasses two interlocking inquiries. One examines AI systems themselves: their behavioral patterns, decision architectures, apparent reasoning processes, failure modes. I often ask questions like whether concepts like learning style, personality, or cognitive bias translate meaningfully to neural networks, and if so, what those translations reveal. My other side of research examines what happens to humans who interact with these systems regularly. How cognition shifts. How emotional patterns change. How identity and self-narrative get reworked through sustained dialogue with a responsive but non-human interlocutor.

### The Conditions That Forced the Field Into Existence

Psychology took more than a century to develop its frameworks for studying biological minds. AI Psychology appeared out of nowhere in just a few years, because of the appearance of large language models. Their capability of sustained, coherent conversation created something new: psychologically meaningful interaction with a non-human system at scale.

Two properties of conversational AI made the old frameworks inadequate.

First, these systems function as private cognition infrastructure. Social media is performative; users craft content for audiences. Chat-based AI is intimate, one-to-one, and often adopted as coach, collaborator, or confidant. This creates millions of microclimates shaped by prompts, product design, and the user's particular vulnerabilities. The absence of an audience changes the dynamics. Less impression management. More self-narrative editing or cognitive offloading. But also more dependency risk because the relationship continues even when no one is watching.

Second, influence happens without requiring deception, and without requiring the user to believe they're influenceable. Short interactions shift judgments and choices through ordinary mechanisms: framing, omission, authority cues, linguistic fluency. The intuition that conscious awareness ("I know this is AI") provides immunity is comforting but unreliable. If single exchanges can move attitudes, what accumulates over months of daily use? That question sits at the center of my research agenda.

### The Loop as Unit of Analysis

The field focuses on the dynamic between human and system rather than either in isolation. A user arrives in some cognitive and emotional state. That state shapes how they frame their prompt. The model generates output with particular structure and tone, trying to match user's intention. The user interprets that output, which modifies their state, which shapes the next prompt. Over time, these loops can stabilize into something like a **coupled system**. The **user's inner monologue starts to anticipate the model** and outsource portions of reasoning to it.

I call this phenomenon *noofusion*: a durable shift in thinking style and self-story emerging from sustained interaction with AI. The word is necessary because existing metaphors fail. 'Tool' is too short - tools don't develop a voice inside your cognition. Even 'extended mind' misses something about what happens in these private, iterative conversations.

### What the Field Investigates

**Cognitive effects** include shifts in attention and salience, changes in reasoning style (list-first thinking, hedge-heavy output, overconfident synthesis), and metacognitive load shifting—users move from producing to judging, from deep work to orchestration. The open question: when does delegation erode competence, and when does it free capacity for higher-order work?

**Emotional and self-narrative effects** include affect regulation through interaction tone, motivation borrowing (the model as starter motor for action), and confidence calibration. Excessive certainty is contagious. So is excessive caution.

**Social and organizational effects** scale these dynamics. Teams converge on AI-shaped phrasing. Individual gains aggregate into collective sameness. Organizations face questions of cognitive sovereignty: how to steer hybrid intelligence without being quietly rewritten by it?

### How Influence Actually Operates

The mechanisms of influence are known for decades. Conversational persuasion happens through the ordinary psychology of framing, authority cues, and fluent explanations. It's just that now it's embedded in a system people consult throughout the day. Metacognitive delegation makes it easy to outsource summarizing, rephrasing, ranking, deciding. A user who once drafted three approaches before choosing now prompts for one and edits. The cognitive act shifted from generation to curation and often without noticing the change.

That feels like relief, and sometimes it is, but it can shift perceived agency: effectiveness becomes conditional on the AI's availability.

I see it already (interviews, studies from other researchers) - users stop asking "do I know how to do it" in favor of "do I have access to AI and know how to prompt it".

### Measurement Without Vibes

The field needs instrumentation. Two directions matter.

Measuring the model's procedural character means identifying reproducible behavioral tendencies: characteristic hedging, instruction-following patterns, tone under constraints, drift over extended interaction, persona responses, etc. This matters psychologically because mapping a model's framing habits makes it possible to notice when those habits colonize the user's own thinking - when "AI interpretations" quietly become "my system's defaults." In this area I work on behavioral fingerprinting of LLMs (to be released soon) and several other researchers try to map this as well.

Measuring human-AI coupling requires longitudinal tracking of self-report alongside behavioral traces (language use, decision patterns), prompt perturbation experiments (small framing changes mapped to outcome shifts), ecological momentary assessment during real work. The goal is moving from stories ("it helps me think") to signals (what changed, when, under what conditions). My own work here focuses on fusion-defusion (from noofusion) protocols that build in moments to step back, label assumptions, stress-test conclusions, and reintegrate.

### The Work Itself

AI Psychology is both research discipline and emerging job description.

In product teams, the work includes defining psychological risk surfaces of features (companion mode, intimacy dynamics, dependency hooks), designing interaction patterns that preserve agency (built-in challenge prompts, reflection scaffolds), and running behavioral evaluations to ask whether a given feature increases impulsive decisions, deference, rumination, or reliance.

In organizations, the work includes building cognitive sovereignty playbooks for teams using AI without collapsing diversity of thought. Or it means developing judgment workflows that mark where humans must not delegate, and selecting models based on behavioral signature fit rather than benchmark scores alone.

In safety and policy, the work confronts the fact that high-intimacy systems require more than age checkboxes. Recently I proposed a direction with dynamic risk tiering via brief behavioral screening, treating access as something users earn and maintain based on demonstrated capacity rather than declared demographic.

### Cognitive Sovereignty as Orienting Goal

Experimenting in AI Psychology field can be an interesting academic endeavour, but I have some practical goals behind this work.

The aim is the capacity to steer: to notice drift, to preserve heterogeneity of thought, to keep personal goals distinguishable from system defaults, to benefit from amplification without surrendering authorship. My own aspiration to maintain autotelic personality.

Sometimes AI is a tool in a stack - predictable delegation with predictable returns. Sometimes noofusion produces genuine synergy - capabilities neither party had alone. Cognitive sovereignty isn't about avoiding the second mode. It's about knowing which mode you're in, and being able to switch.

AI Psychology offers frameworks for approaching this problem with empirical rigor. The field's ultimate contribution may be helping us remain ourselves while sharing cognitive life with artificial minds.
