Summary: The paper introduces Handroid, a compact, desktop-scale robot that can reconfigure itself into either a dexterous anthropomorphic hand or a functional humanoid robot. This dual embodiment aims to bridge the gap between contact-rich manipulation capabilities of dexterous hands and the mobility and whole-body interaction of humanoids. Handroid features a 27-Degree-of-Freedom (DoF) body, with 20 DoFs dedicated to an anthropomorphic hand in its hand configuration and these modules reconfigured to form a humanoid (head, arms, legs) with a 12-DoF lower-limb structure for locomotion in its humanoid configuration. The platform supports a unified control and learning framework for teleoperation, grasping, in-hand manipulation, locomotion, gait generation, and motion authoring. Its capabilities are validated through real-world demonstrations of dexterous manipulation, reinforcement learning-based locomotion, and a long-horizon task involving embodiment reconfiguration and pick-and-place operations.
Key Findings:
- Introduction of Handroid: a dual-embodiment, reconfigurable robot platform.
- Handroid can switch between a dexterous hand and a desktop humanoid form.
- Integrates capabilities for both contact-rich manipulation and whole-body interaction.
- Features a 27-DoF electromechanical body.
- Provides a unified control and learning framework for diverse robotic tasks.
- Validated through real-world experiments including locomotion and manipulation.
- Positions Handroid as a platform for advancing morphology-reconfigurable robotics and cross-embodiment robot learning.
