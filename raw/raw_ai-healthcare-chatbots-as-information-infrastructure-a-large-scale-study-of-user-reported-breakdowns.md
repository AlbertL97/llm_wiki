Title: AI Healthcare Chatbots as Information Infrastructure: A Large-Scale Study of User-Reported Breakdowns

Summary:
This paper presents a large-scale study analyzing over 15,000 user reviews from 59 AI healthcare chatbot applications. The research aims to understand the performance and impact of these chatbots in everyday informational and emotional contexts within healthcare.

Methodology:
- Topic modeling and interpretive analysis were used on user reviews.

Key Findings:
Three recurring categories of breakdowns were identified:
1.  **Access Barriers and Service Unreliability:** Issues related to users being unable to access the service or the service not functioning reliably.
2.  **User Experience and Interaction Quality:** Problems concerning how users interact with the chatbot, including usability, clarity, and the overall experience of the conversation.
3.  **Billing and Customer Support Issues:** Difficulties related to financial aspects and user support when problems arise.

Specific Concerns:
-   **Privacy and Security:** These concerns were strongly associated with the most negative user experiences, indicating a significant impact on user trust and satisfaction.

Framing:
-   The study frames AI healthcare chatbots as **information infrastructures**.

Implications:
-   Failures in access, usability, and trust negatively affect users.
-   Provides actionable insights for designers, policymakers, and information professionals to improve digital health systems.

Contribution:
-   Offers a large-scale empirical perspective on the real-world performance and user-reported failures of AI healthcare chatbots.
-   Emphasizes the need for robust information infrastructure to support digital health.
