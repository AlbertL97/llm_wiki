# Data Standards for Humanoid Robotics: The Missing Infrastructure for Physical AI

**Authors:** N/A (ArXiv preprint)
**Date:** 2026-06-17 (v1)

## Main Findings:

- **Scalability of humanoid robots depends on data standards:** Beyond models and hardware, the ability for physical experience to accumulate across robots, tasks, organizations, and time is crucial for scalability.
- **Humanoid robot data is embodied interaction data:** It's not just isolated digital samples, but must preserve the relationships between robot body, action, task, scene, execution trace, and outcome.
- **Physical coherence is essential for reusability:** Multimodal data streams are reusable only if timing, coordinate frames, calibration, kinematics, units, and synchronization assumptions are inspectable.
- **Bottlenecks in data accumulation:** High collection costs, data silos, and inconsistent evaluation lead to non-cumulative data, not just data scarcity.
- **Data standards address bottlenecks:** They make embodied experience interpretable, shareable, traceable, and reusable.
- **Standardization scope:** A general standard should cover lifecycle management, metadata, provenance, quality, versioning, and traceability. Capability-specific parts should define domain grammar for manipulation, locomotion, human-robot interaction, cognition, and future capabilities.
- **Evolution of data standards:** As AI moves from screens to bodies, data standards must shift from organizing digital information to structuring physical interaction.
