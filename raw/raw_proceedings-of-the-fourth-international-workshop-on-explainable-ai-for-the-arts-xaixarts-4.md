**Summary:**

The Fourth International Workshop on eXplainable AI for the Arts (XAIxArts 4) convenes researchers and creative practitioners from fields such as Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Interaction Design, AI, and Digital Arts. The workshop aims to deepen the understanding and application of Explainable AI (XAI) within artistic contexts. XAI is presented as a crucial component of Responsible and Human-Centred AI, focusing on making AI models more transparent and comprehensible to users. 

XAIxArts offers a unique perspective by examining explainability through the lens of creative and artistic domains. The previous iterations of the workshop have explored the evolving landscape and speculative futures of AI in creative processes. For XAIxArts 4, the focus shifts to the practical implementation and operationalization of XAI in the Arts. 

Key objectives and activities of the workshop include:

*   **Critical Reflection on Emerging Practices:** Examining and reflecting upon new practices that promote diversity and inclusivity within the field of XAI.
*   **Ideation of Missing Projects:** Collectively brainstorming and identifying potential research gaps and future collaboration opportunities to stimulate new projects.
*   **Development of a Resource Hub:** Scoping and planning for the creation of a central resource hub for open XAIxArts projects. This hub will serve to archive tangible XAI interventions and facilitate community building, contributing to the broader discourse on Human-Centred AI.

The workshop emphasizes making opaque AI models understandable to people, a core tenet of HCI, and uses artistic and creative domains as a distinctive lens to explore these explainability challenges.
