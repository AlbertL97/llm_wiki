In China’s Chengdu, robots are moving out of labs to show what they can really do as the city yesterday launched its first real-world pilot program for intelligent robots.  
  
At a busy downtown intersection, a humanoid robot is now helping traffic police direct cars and guide pedestrians across the road without needing an internet connection.  
  
At a popular tourist spot, a holographic smart screen displays ancient China’s Three Kingdoms(220-280 AD) characters in naked-eye 3D. Also, at Wu Hou Shrine Museum, visitors can chat with a holographic guide or wear AR smart glasses for real-time translation, perfect for international tourists.  
  
Meanwhile at a Primary School, a playful robot named Xiao Zha, styled after traditional folk hero Nezha, is winning over students with voice-guided safety tips.  
  
Robot dogs are now patrolling Chengdu’s busy streets, quietly scanning their surroundings and learning as they go.  
  
The city aims to grow its AI core industry to over 130 billion yuan by 2025 with an annual growth rate exceeding 30%.
