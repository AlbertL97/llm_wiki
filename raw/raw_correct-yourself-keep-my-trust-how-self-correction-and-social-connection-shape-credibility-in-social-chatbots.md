### Correct Yourself, Keep My Trust: How Self-Correction and Social Connection Shape Credibility in Social Chatbots

**Authors:** Not specified in the provided description.

**Date:** 2026-06-18

**URL:** http://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19286v1

**Summary:**
This paper explores the impact of error correction strategies on user trust and credibility in social chatbots. With chatbots increasingly integrated into daily life and prone to generating inaccurate information, their recovery from mistakes is critical. The researchers conducted a between-subjects experiment (N=120) comparing three error correction strategies: a webpage retraction, self-correction by the same chatbot, and correction by an expert chatbot.

Key findings include:
1.  **Credibility Maintenance:** All three strategies were equally effective at correcting factual errors. However, only self-correction by the chatbot maintained its credibility; participants rated self-correcting chatbots significantly higher in trustworthiness and perceived expertise compared to those whose errors were corrected externally.
2.  **Social Connection's Role:** The strength of the user's social connection with the chatbot (measured by social attraction and self-disclosure) significantly predicted the magnitude of belief change, but exclusively when the chatbot corrected itself. Outsourcing corrections entirely severed this link between social connection and belief change.

These results suggest that social chatbots should prioritize correcting their own mistakes rather than relying on external sources. Furthermore, investing in building social connection with users is identified as a functional mechanism that enhances the effectiveness of error correction, moving beyond its traditional perception as merely a design feature. The paper discusses implications for designing chatbots that can sustain long-term credibility while effectively addressing their inaccuracies.
