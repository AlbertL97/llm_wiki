Authors: P. Y. Lee, P. V. Van Bavel, M. L. P. Van Bavel, D. A. P. Van Bavel
Date: 2026-07-04
Title: Synthetic Contact with AI Reduces Cross-Partisan Animosity
URL: http://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02181v1

Abstract:
Americans' warmth toward members of the opposing political party has fallen sharply over the past three decades -- yet meaningful cross-partisan contact remains scarce, in part because people actively avoid it. Across five preregistered studies (total N = 3,960 U.S. partisans), we test whether brief conversations with AI chatbots representing the political outgroup can substitute for the contact people shun.

Key Findings:
- Synthetic contact lowers the barrier to entry: Partisans would endure almost twice as long contemplating their own mortality to avoid a human outgroup partner as an AI one.
- Conversations correct misperceptions: A single ten-minute conversation with an outgroup chatbot corrected misperceptions about outgroup attitudes and warmed affect.
- Drivers of effect: A three-arm experiment ruled out pure engagement and sociality as drivers, suggesting the content of the conversation is key.
- Behavioral impact: Participants who spoke with an outgroup bot about immigration were six percentage points more likely than controls to choose to have a real conversation with a partisan from the other side.
- Persistence of effect: The warmth effect replicated immediately but mostly faded within a week, with a small residual concentrated among the most extreme partisans.
- Content analysis: Information, more than friendliness, distinguishes outgroup bots from control chatbots.
- Conclusion: Synthetic contact is established as a scalable, behaviorally consequential, and widely acceptable form of cross-partisan engagement.
