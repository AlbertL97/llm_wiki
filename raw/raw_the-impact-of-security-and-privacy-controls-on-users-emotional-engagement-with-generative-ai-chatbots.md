Summary:
This research explores the impact of security and privacy (S&P) controls on users' emotional engagement and willingness to disclose sensitive information to generative AI chatbots, particularly in the context of emotional support and companionship.

Key Findings:
- Generative AI chatbots are increasingly used for emotional support and companionship.
- S&P controls, such as model training opt-outs and memory toggles, are available but their influence on sensitive disclosure is understudied.
- A mixed-methods vignette study with 354 U.S. participants examined the effects of S&P controls on willingness to engage, perceived protection, and perceived chatbot effectiveness for emotional support.
- Controls that enabled deletion of disclosures had the most significant positive impact on user engagement and willingness to disclose.
- Technically sophisticated controls (e.g., local-only processing, model training opt-outs) were less impactful, largely due to users' difficulty understanding their underlying mechanisms.
- Despite the presence of controls, user trust remained fragile, with participants often doubting the efficacy of these controls.
- The study concludes with recommendations for bridging user comprehension gaps, building credible assurances, and calibrating S&P barriers for users in distress.

Methodology:
Mixed-methods vignette study with 354 U.S. participants.

Implications:
- Design of S&P controls should prioritize user comprehension and clear communication over technical sophistication.
- Building trust in AI for sensitive applications requires more than just implementing controls; it necessitates demonstrating their reliability and transparency.
- Special considerations are needed for users in distress, who may be more vulnerable to privacy concerns and less able to navigate complex S&P settings.
