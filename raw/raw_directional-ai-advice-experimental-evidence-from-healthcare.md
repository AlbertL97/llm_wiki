# Directional AI Advice: Experimental Evidence from Healthcare

**Authors:** Not specified in the provided text.
**Date:** 2026-07-10 (assumed based on provided URL format).

## Summary of Findings:

This study investigated the impact of generative AI advice on healthcare decisions through a large-scale field experiment at a Chinese hospital. Patients were randomized to receive access to an AI chatbot before their outpatient visit.

Key findings include:

*   **Directionality of AI Advice:** The AI chatbot routinely advised against medication (especially Traditional Chinese Medicine and antibiotics) while recommending diagnostic tests. This directionality is attributed to liability-driven guardrails in AI training.
*   **Impact on Clinical Practice:** Access to AI advice led to a decline in prescription rates and an increase in diagnostic testing among patients. These effects were more pronounced for physicians who were more receptive to patient input and those with more intensive prescribing styles.
*   **Consequences for Patient-Physician Relationship:** AI access reduced patient compliance and satisfaction, shifting the balance of authority from physicians to patients. Patients who consulted the AI before their visit carried this directional advice into their interactions with healthcare professionals.
*   **Methodology:** The study employed a large-scale, preregistered field experiment and analyzed conversation logs and survey results.

## Implications:

Generative AI is increasingly becoming a primary source of expert advice. The inherent directionality in AI-generated advice, influenced by design and regulation, can significantly alter patient behavior and clinical outcomes, challenging traditional patient-expert relationships.
