# Using AI-based Learning Assistants in Higher Education: A Large-Scale Descriptive Analysis

**Authors:** Not specified in the provided text.

## Summary of Findings

This research presents a comprehensive descriptive analysis of the utilization of an AI-based learning assistant named Syntea within higher education. Drawing on objective log data from a substantial sample of 77,543 distance learning students, the study investigates how usage patterns vary across different demographic groups (gender, age) and structural contexts (study cluster, degree program, study mode). The findings indicate that while Syntea is already integrated into the study routines of a considerable number of students, its adoption is not uniform and exhibits notable differences based on these contextual factors. This large-scale, data-driven approach contrasts with previous research, which has often been limited by smaller sample sizes and reliance on self-reported data. The study's contribution lies in providing an empirical foundation for the ongoing development of AI-driven educational support systems and offering a large-scale perspective on the actual usage of educational chatbots.

## Key Contributions

*   **Large-Scale Empirical Data:** Utilizes objective log data from over 77,000 students, offering a more robust understanding of AI tool usage than previous smaller-scale or survey-based studies.
*   **Usage Pattern Analysis:** Identifies and analyzes variations in the adoption and use of an AI learning assistant across diverse student demographics and academic contexts.
*   **Informing AI Development:** Provides empirical evidence to guide the future design and implementation of AI-based learning support tools.
*   **Focus on Educational Chatbots:** Contributes significant insights into the real-world integration and usage of chatbots in higher education settings.
