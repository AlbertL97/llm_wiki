**Title:** Invisible Impact of Empathy on Behavioral Change: Isolating the Effect of Empathy in Long-term Physical Activity Coaching Chatbot Interactions

**Summary:**
This paper explores the role of empathy in AI-driven chatbots designed for long-term physical activity (PA) coaching, specifically focusing on its impact on behavioral change. The researchers developed three WhatsApp PA coaching chatbots that differed solely in their level of empathy. These chatbots were evaluated in a six-week within-subject study with 13 participants.

A key finding was that participants had difficulty distinguishing between the different empathy conditions. Surprisingly, the non-empathetic chatbot was often perceived as more engaging and useful by users.

Despite the lack of explicit identification, the study observed that the higher-empathy chatbot variants were correlated with a larger average increase in participants' step counts and a quicker improvement in their intention to adhere to advice. This suggests that while users may not consciously recognize or articulate the empathy presented, it can still exert a subtle influence on motivation and build trust, thereby supporting sustained behavioral modification.

The authors interpret these findings through the lens of the Elaboration Likelihood Model, specifically the peripheral route of persuasion. They highlight the need for designers to consider a balance between effectiveness and human-like connection when developing next-generation PA coaching chatbots.
