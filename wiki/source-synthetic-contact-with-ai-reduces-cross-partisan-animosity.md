# Synthetic Contact with AI Reduces Cross-Partisan Animosity

**Last updated:** 2026-07-04

## Main Findings

This research explores the potential of using AI chatbots to facilitate positive cross-partisan interactions, aiming to mitigate the growing animosity between political groups in the United States. The study found that engaging in brief conversations with AI chatbots representing the opposing political party can significantly reduce partisan animosity. Key findings indicate that synthetic contact not only lowers the initial barrier to engagement, as individuals are more willing to interact with an AI than a human outgroup member, but also corrects misperceptions about the outgroup's attitudes. A single ten-minute conversation was sufficient to improve affective warmth and accuracy of beliefs. The study further demonstrated that the positive effects were driven by the informational content of the AI's responses rather than mere social interaction or friendliness. Importantly, synthetic contact influenced behavior, making participants more likely to seek out real-world conversations with members of the opposing party. While the immediate positive effects on warmth diminished within a week, a residual effect persisted, particularly among more extreme partisans. The findings establish synthetic contact as a scalable and impactful method for cross-partisan engagement.

## Key Findings

*   **Reduced barrier to interaction:** Participants were significantly more willing to engage with an AI chatbot representing the outgroup compared to a human outgroup member.
*   **Correction of misperceptions:** Brief conversations with outgroup AI chatbots led to more accurate perceptions of the outgroup's attitudes and beliefs.
*   **Warming of affect:** Synthetic contact resulted in increased warmth towards the political outgroup.
*   **Behavioral influence:** Participants who interacted with an outgroup chatbot were more inclined to engage in real-world cross-partisan conversations.
*   **Mechanism of effect:** The study suggests that the informational content exchanged during the conversation, rather than just social engagement or friendliness, is the primary driver of the observed effects.
*   **Durability of effects:** While immediate positive effects on warmth were observed, they largely faded within a week, with a smaller, persistent effect among highly partisan individuals.
