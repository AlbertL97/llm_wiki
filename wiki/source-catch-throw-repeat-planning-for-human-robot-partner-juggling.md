# Catch, Throw, Repeat: Planning for Human-Robot Partner Juggling

**Authors:** Kai Plöger, David K. Lee, Laura Fehrenbach, Florian L. Schuster, Andreas

**Date:** 2026-07-15 (as per arXiv submission date, though the request date is 2026-07-17)

## Summary

This article introduces a novel real-time planning and control system designed to enable robots to engage in partner juggling with humans. The system addresses the complexities of dynamic object exchange, incorporating predictive ball tracking, adaptive trajectory optimization, and state-machine-based coordination logic. A user study involving 8 participants demonstrated the system's effectiveness in synchronized three-ball cascade juggling, with participants achieving significantly better results than previously reported records. This work represents a significant advancement in physical human-robot interaction and shared autonomy.

## Key Findings

*   **Developed a real-time planning and control architecture for human-robot partner juggling.** This system allows robots to reliably catch and throw balls in synchronized multi-ball patterns with human partners.
*   **Integrated key components for robust interaction:** predictive ball tracking, adaptive online trajectory optimization (using a multiple-shooting formulation), and state-machine-based coordination logic.
*   **Demonstrated system effectiveness in a user study:** 8 participants with varying juggling skills successfully performed three-ball cascades with the robot.
*   **Achieved record-breaking performance:** Participants exceeded previous best-case results within a 10-minute session, with notable individual achievements in both multi-ball and single-ball catch-and-return scenarios.
*   **Highlights the potential for advancing physical human-robot interaction and shared autonomy.**
