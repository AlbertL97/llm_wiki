# Directional AI Advice: Experimental Evidence from Healthcare

## Overview

This paper presents findings from a large-scale, preregistered field experiment conducted in a Chinese hospital to evaluate the impact of generative AI advice on patient-physician interactions within the healthcare domain. The study examines how patients' pre-consultation use of an AI chatbot influences their subsequent clinical encounters, treatment decisions, and satisfaction.

## Key Findings

*   **AI Bias in Advice:** The AI chatbot systematically provided directional advice, cautioning against medication use (particularly Traditional Chinese Medicine and antibiotics) while promoting diagnostic testing. This bias is linked to the "liability-driven guardrails" embedded in the AI's training.
*   **Shift in Healthcare Utilization:** Patients who accessed the AI chatbot before their doctor's visit exhibited lower prescription rates and higher rates of diagnostic testing compared to those who did not.
*   **Differential Physician Impact:** The observed effects on healthcare utilization were more pronounced among physicians who were more receptive to patient input and those who had a more intensive prescribing style, suggesting AI influences existing physician practices.
*   **Erosion of Patient Compliance and Satisfaction:** AI access was associated with reduced patient compliance with medical advice and lower overall satisfaction with the healthcare experience.
*   **Altered Authority Dynamics:** The interaction with AI shifted the balance of power and authority between patients and physicians, with patients entering consultations armed with AI-generated advice that influenced the subsequent clinical judgment.

## Methodology

The research utilized a randomized controlled trial design, with patients assigned to either receive access to an AI chatbot prior to their medical appointment or not. Analysis included examination of conversation logs from the AI interactions, clinical data on treatment choices, and survey data on patient compliance and satisfaction.
