## Main content

### Overview
This study ($N=178$ older adults) translates, validates, and applies the Polish version of the **Godspeed Questionnaire Series (GQS)** to assess the perception of the social robot TIAGo in care settings (source: The Godspeed Questionnaire Series in the Assessment of the Social Robot TIAGo by Older Individuals.md).

### The Five GQS Dimensions
The GQS measures robot perception across five key series:
- **S1: Anthropomorphism**: Perceived human-likeness.
- **S2: Animacy**: Perceived lifelikeness/purposeful movement.
- **S3: Likeability**: User affection and comfort.
- **S4: Perceived Intelligence**: Perceived cognitive capability.
- **S5: Perceived Safety**: Perceived safety and predictability.

### Major Findings
1. **Reliability**: The Polish version demonstrated high internal consistency (overall Cronbach's $lpha = 0.90$).
2. **Independence of Acceptance**: Perceived anthropomorphism and animacy scored significantly lower than likeability and intelligence. Crucially, **user acceptance of the robot does not correlate with perceived anthropomorphism or animacy**. Older adults value safety and intelligence (utility) far more than human-likeness.
3. **Residency Effect**: Institutionalized older adults scored likeability (S3) and safety (S5) significantly higher than those living independently in the community, suggesting different support needs and isolation levels affect social robot appraisal (source: The Godspeed Questionnaire Series in the Assessment of the Social Robot TIAGo by Older Individuals.md).
