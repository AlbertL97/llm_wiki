## Main content

### Overview
This systematic literature review examines psychophysiological measures used to assess human **mental workload** during human-robot collaboration (HRC) in industrial (Industry 5.0) and service settings (source: Capturing Mental Workload Through Physiological Sensors in Human-Robot Collaboration_ A Systematic Literature Review.md).

### Psychophysiological Modalities
1. **Electroencephalography (EEG)**: Directly indexes cognitive processing load, particularly through theta band activity in frontal regions and alpha band activity in parietal regions.
2. **Ocular Metrics (Eye-tracking/Pupillometry)**: Pupillary dilation and fixation duration correlate strongly with sudden increases in task difficulty and information-processing load.
3. **Autonomic Metrics (ECG/EDA)**: Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and Electrodermal Activity (skin conductance) capture stress, physiological arousal, and physical fatigue.
4. **Key Recommendations**: The authors highlight a critical lack of consensus on how to operationalize mental workload and call for standardized physiological baseline protocols to improve the accuracy of adaptive HRC systems (source: Capturing Mental Workload Through Physiological Sensors in Human-Robot Collaboration_ A Systematic Literature Review.md).
