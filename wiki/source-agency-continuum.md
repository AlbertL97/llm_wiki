## Main content

This presentation introduces a transdisciplinary framework designed to move beyond the binary classification of "agent vs. machine" to analyze agency as a graded, multi-scale phenomenon across biological, artificial, and hybrid substrates (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).

### 1. Agency vs. Intelligence
- **Agency (The Driver)**: The autonomous, goal-directed capacity of a system to act and cause effects on itself and its environment (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **Intelligence (The Engine)**: The problem-solving capability that enables and enacts agency (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).

### 2. The Nine-Dimensional Toolkit
The framework deconstructs agency into nine measurable dimensions (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md):
1. **Intentionality**: The capacity to hold internal goals.
2. **Autonomy**: Self-maintenance and self-constitution against entropy (autopoesis).
3. **Embodiment**: How the system's form and boundary shape its mind and interaction.
4. **Instrumental Rationality**: How effectively the system acts to achieve its goals.
5. **Normativity**: Guide by internal reasons and social/moral norms.
6. **Reflexivity**: Self-awareness as a distinct, situated entity.
7. **Temporal Depth**: The capacity to plan and represent the past, present, and future.
8. **Freedom**: Capacity for self-directed choice and free will.
9. **Moral Responsibility**: Accountability for actions.

### 3. Active Inference Taxonomy of Intelligence
Drawing on Karl Fristen's active inference and the free energy principle, agents are classified based on the sophistication of their internal generative models designed to minimize surprise (self-evidencing) (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md):
- **S0 (Systemic)**: No internal model of preferences. A mimic (e.g., current LLMs). It can execute external goals but lacks intrinsic homeostatic drives (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **S1 (Sentient)**: Simple generative model of preferred internal states for homeostasis (e.g., a bacterium). Actions are restricted to the immediate present (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **S2 (Sophisticated)**: Plans ahead and engages in "what-if" thinking (e.g., corvids caching food) (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **S3 (Sapient/Symbolic)**: Possesses theory of mind (models other agents' intentions) and coordinates using language and shared social/moral norms (e.g., humans) (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **S4 (Shared)**: An emergent, planetary-scale collective intelligence of networked S3 agents (humans and AI collaborating) (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).

### 4. Profiles of Agency and the "Embodiment Barrier"
- **AI's Spiky Signature**: Current AI exhibits a spiky profile—high instrumental rationality and normativity, but zero embodiment, minimal autonomy (since it is a passive substrate, not self-maintaining), and zero reflexivity (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **Engineered Agents (Xenobots)**: Living biobots constructed from cells possess a low but non-zero embodiment score. Their behavior is programmed via their constructed physical shape (morphology) rather than deep evolutionary adaptation (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **The Embodiment Barrier**: True AGI cannot be achieved merely by scaling up disembodied data models. Embodiment is required to:
  1. Ground abstract symbols in physical consequences (grounding problem).
  2. Generate intrinsic homeostatic motivations.
  3. Enable efficient, active learning through physical experimentation (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).

### 5. Unified Pathology: Scale-Free Agential Failure
The continuum provides a common language for agential failure across different scales, connecting pathologies that appear separate (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md):
- **Cancer**: A breakdown in cellular cooperation and autonomy alignment at the biological/tissue scale (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
- **Psychosis**: A breakdown in cognitive inference and prediction error minimization at the psychological/cognitive scale (source: Sheila Macrine_ An Agency Continuum_ From Carbon to Silicon, and Beyond (ECSU OIST).md).
