# Proceedings of The Fourth International Workshop on eXplainable AI for the Arts (XAIxArts 4)

This document outlines the proceedings of the Fourth International Workshop on eXplainable AI for the Arts (XAIxArts 4). The workshop brings together a diverse community of researchers and practitioners from HCI, AI, Interaction Design, and Digital Arts to explore the critical role of Explainable AI (XAI) in artistic applications.

## Key Focus Areas and Objectives

*   **Explainable AI (XAI) in the Arts:** The workshop's central theme is the application and operationalization of XAI within creative and artistic domains, aiming to make opaque AI models more understandable to individuals.
*   **Human-Centred AI:** XAI is positioned as a fundamental aspect of Responsible and Human-Centred AI, emphasizing HCI techniques for increased transparency.
*   **Community Building and Collaboration:** The event aims to foster a community of practice by encouraging critical reflection on emerging XAI practices, ideating future projects, and developing a resource hub for open XAIxArts projects.
*   **Diversity and Inclusivity:** A specific focus is placed on critically reflecting on practices that promote diversity and inclusivity within XAI development and application.

## Relevance to Psychology of AI/Robot Interaction

While not directly focused on psychological constructs like trust or mental health, the emphasis on making AI understandable through XAI has significant implications for **[[human-ai-interaction]]** and potentially **[[trust]]** in AI systems, especially as AI becomes more integrated into creative and expressive fields. The focus on human-centeredness also aligns with broader discussions in HCI that intersect with user psychology.
