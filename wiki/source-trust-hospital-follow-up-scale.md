## Main content

### Overview
This study describes the development and validation of a specialized **Artificial Intelligence-Based Follow-Up Trust Scale** designed to measure patient trust in automated follow-up systems (source: Trust in artificial intelligence-based follow-up in hospital information systems_ development and validation of a new scale.md).

### Scale Structure
The final validated instrument consists of **27 items** divided into three core dimensions:
1. **Patient Dispositional Trust**: The patient's pre-existing, general propensity to trust technology and medical systems.
2. **System Interaction Trust**: Perceived reliability, accuracy, and usability of the AI follow-up system during interaction.
3. **Environmental Trust**: Perceived safety, institutional support, and privacy guarantees within the hospital information system context.

### Psychometric Properties
- **Validity**: Content validity was high (I-CVI: 0.833–1.000, S-CVI/Ave: 0.955). Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA) confirmed a three-factor solution explaining 66.0% of the variance. Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA) verified a strong model fit ($\chi^2/df = 1.096$, RMSEA = 0.022, CFI = 0.999, TLI = 0.99).
- **Reliability**: High internal consistency (Cronbach's $lpha = 0.931$, McDonald's $\omega = 0.960$) and strong test-retest reliability ($r = 0.901$). This scale provides a standardized tool to assess and improve patient adherence to automated post-discharge care (source: Trust in artificial intelligence-based follow-up in hospital information systems_ development and validation of a new scale.md).
