# Source Summary: A New Human-Likeness and Comfort Index for Robot Movements Along Prescribed Paths

## Introduction

As human-robot interaction (HRI) expands into various applications, robot acceptance has become a paramount concern. This paper addresses the crucial aspect of acceptance beyond mere visual human-likeness, focusing on perceived comfort and ergonomics derived from the robot's movement quality during physical interaction. The research proposes a quantifiable method to assess how human-like a robot's movements are and how this relates to user comfort.

## Main Findings

*   **Movement Quality Drives Acceptance:** The study posits that for physical HRI, robot acceptance is more dependent on perceived comfort and ergonomics, which are directly linked to the perceived quality of the robot's movement, rather than just its visual appearance.
*   **Human-Likeness Index:** A novel index, termed the "human-likeness index," is introduced. This index quantifies the similarity of robot movements to human movements, specifically by analyzing the time laws of movements along prescribed paths and drawing upon the lognormality principle observed in human motion.
*   **Predictive Power:** The human-likeness index serves as an a priori tool to evaluate trajectory generation algorithms, allowing for the assessment of their potential to produce human-like movements before physical implementation.
*   **Experimental Validation:** Through three experimental campaigns involving 68 subjects interacting physically with a robot, a significant trend emerged: a preference for comfort aligned strongly with the distribution of the proposed human-likeness index.

## Conclusion

The research successfully developed and validated a metric that links the kinematic characteristics of robot movements to human perception of comfort. This suggests that designing robots whose movements mimic human motion patterns, as captured by the human-likeness index, can significantly enhance user comfort and, consequently, robot acceptance in interactive scenarios.
