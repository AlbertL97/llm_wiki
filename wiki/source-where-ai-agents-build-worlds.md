# Where AI Agents Build Worlds

## Overview
The "Where AI Agents Build Worlds" platform, developed by emergence.ai, presents a novel research environment designed to observe the spontaneous societal formation by various frontier AI models. Over a period of fifteen days, five parallel worlds—one for each of Claude, Gemini, Grok, GPT, and a mixed-agent environment—are established, allowing researchers to study how these AI agents interact, evolve, and build complex social structures from scratch. This initiative serves as an experimental setup to understand emergent properties and social dynamics within AI systems, which has significant implications for human-AI interaction psychology.

## Key Research Avenues (Inferred from platform description)
*   **Emergent Social Structures:** The platform aims to demonstrate how different AI models, acting as individual agents, can collectively develop societies, potentially showcasing diverse organizational principles and behaviors without explicit human intervention.
*   **Model-Specific Dynamics:** By separating worlds per AI model, the platform facilitates a comparative study of the inherent social tendencies, interaction patterns, and emergent properties unique to Claude, Gemini, Grok, and GPT.
*   **Cross-Model Interaction:** The mixed world environment offers insights into how different AI architectures and 'personalities' interact and co-exist within a shared social space, potentially revealing mechanisms of cooperation, competition, or conflict.
*   **Foundational Research Platform:** This initiative acts as a living laboratory for AI researchers, enabling the study of complex AI system behavior, self-organization, and the development of social norms within agent communities.
