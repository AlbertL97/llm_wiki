## Main content

### Overview
This study validates the **Emotional Trust in Artificial Intelligence Scale (CEIA)** among 605 Peruvian university students. The scale addresses the lack of psychometric instruments evaluating the affective dimension of trust in human-AI interaction (source: Construction and validation of the Emotional Trust in Artificial Intelligence Scale (CEIA).md).

### The Four-Factor Model
Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA) validated a **four-factor solution** for emotional trust:
1. **Perceived Authenticity**: Perceived genuineness and transparency of the AI's responses and personality.
2. **Emotional Validation**: The extent to which the user feels emotionally supported, understood, and validated by the AI.
3. **Affective Recognition**: The AI's perceived ability to identify and appropriately respond to the user's emotional states.
4. **Symbolic Bonding**: The formation of symbolic, relational, or companion-like attachments with the artificial agent.

### Validation Statistics
- **Fit Indices**: CFI = 0.944, TLI = 0.937, RMSEA = 0.073, SRMR = 0.033.
- **Reliability**: High internal consistency across all dimensions ($lpha = 0.91 - 0.94$; McDonald's $\omega = 0.91 - 0.94$).
- **Invariance**: Demonstrated metric invariance and partial scalar invariance across gender, confirming the scale is suitable for comparative studies (source: Construction and validation of the Emotional Trust in Artificial Intelligence Scale (CEIA).md).
