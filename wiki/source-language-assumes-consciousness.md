## Main content

This source proposes the grammatical and syntactic blueprints for a non-dual language designed to restructure human cognitive patterns and physical neural pathways around the primacy of consciousness (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).

### 1. Linguistic Relativity and SVO Syntax
- **Whorfian Relativity**: Grammar acts as the cognitive algorithm that consciousness uses to organize sensory chaos into a shared, stable reality (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md). Spoken language structures predispose speakers to habitual patterns of thought.
- **Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) Limits**: Standard Indo-European SVO syntax separates the observer from the observed, placing an actor on one side, an action in the center, and a recipient on the other (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md). Saying *"the stone fell"* grammatically isolates a perceiving agent from an inert physical object.

### 2. Process-Based Grammar and Nominalized Actions
- **Hopi Language Precedent**: The Hopi language lacks linear tenses, tracking duration and validity rather than counting events as segmented units (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).
- **Nominalizing Conscious Acts**: To dismantle SVO subject-object separation, rigid nouns are replaced by nominalized conscious acts (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md). For instance, *"the tree is green"* is reconstructed as *"the greenness is perceived by me, originating from a tree form."*

### 3. E-Prime and the Removal of "To Be"
- **E-Prime Rule**: Derived from Alfred Korzybski's general semantics, E-Prime completely excludes the verb *"to be"* (is, am, are, was, were) (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).
- **Subjective Observation**: Omitting these verbs forces speakers to report events as personal observations rather than absolute, objective truths, reshaping perception of the universe into a continuous subjective event (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).

### 4. Geocentric Spatial decentering and Merged Pronouns
- **Guugu Yimithirr Precedent**: Guugu Yimithirr (Australia) uses a geocentric system (north, south, east, west) rather than egocentric left/right directions, resulting in perfect cardinal memory recall independent of body position (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).
- **Merged Conscious Spaces**: Standard egocentric pronouns (I, you) are replaced by a spectrum of pronouns denoting merged conscious spaces, tracking whether awareness is individual, collective, or universal (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).

### 5. Generative Grammar and Sphoṭa Theory
- **Pāṇini's Rules**: The Aṣṭādhyāyī (500 BCE) operates like a programming language, using ~4,000 rules to process phonetic data and verbal roots through logic gates (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md).
- **Bhartṛhari's Sphoṭa Theory**: Distinguishes between physical sequential speech sounds (*dhvani*) and the instantaneous, indivisible burst of understanding (*sphoṭa*) (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md). Speech sounds act as triggers revealing concepts already present in a shared consciousness.

### 6. Causal Consciousness and Conscious Realism
- **Consciousness as Causal**: Verb conjugations denote the state of awareness (waking, dreaming, transcendent) (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md). Instead of *"the stone fell,"* the grammar becomes *"awareness manifested the falling of the stone,"* identifying conscious observation as the causal force driving the physical universe.
- **Donald Hoffman's Conscious Realism**: Hoffman's mathematical models show that space-time is an evolutionary interface (illusion), replacing physical grids with a dense topological web of interacting conscious agents (source: A Language that Assumes Consciousness is the Basis of Reality.md). Consciousness is defined as integrated information pulsing in recursive loops.
