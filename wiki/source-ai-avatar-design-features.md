## Main content

### Overview
This review synthesizes over two decades of research on trust formation in human-AI avatar and robot interactions, integrating findings from Hancock et al. (2011) and subsequent visual design studies (source: AI Avatar_ Design Features That Drive Trust and Adoption.md).

### Landmark Findings
1. **Dominance of Performance Factors**: Hancock et al. (2011) established that **robot-related factors** (competence, performance, and reliability) have the strongest association with trust ($r = +0.26$), while human factors and environmental factors show smaller effects.
2. **Three Core Domains of Trust**:
   - **Competence & Reliability**: Proven capability of the AI system.
   - **Anthropomorphism & Warmth**: Design features that signal social presence and emotional availability.
   - **Transparency & Predictability**: Clear communication of internal states and limitations.
3. **Visual Design & Trust Perception**:
   - Facial features shape trustworthiness judgments in milliseconds.
   - **Duchenne Smiles**: The integration of micro-smiles (eye crinkles/crow's feet) in avatars/robots significantly increases perceived warmth, benevolence, and trust compared to non-Duchenne smiles.
   - For domestic or helper contexts, abstract or absent eye designs can sometimes reduce the uncanny valley effect and make the robot feel less threatening (source: AI Avatar_ Design Features That Drive Trust and Adoption.md).
