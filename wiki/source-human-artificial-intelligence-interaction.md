## Main content

### Overview
This editorial article outlines the interdisciplinary research landscape of Human-Artificial Intelligence Interaction, focusing on user experience, trust, and professional adaptation (source: On the "Human" in Human-Artificial Intelligence Interaction.md).

### Core Research Areas
1. **Trust and Professional Risk**: Professionals in fields like healthcare and business face career and safety risks if they rely on opaque AI decisions. Trust is not a simple binary but a complex, socio-cognitive calibration process.
2. **Educational Needs**: As AI tools integrate into professional workflows, training must evolve. Education should promote critical thinking and teach professionals how and when to challenge AI recommendations.
3. **UX Design for Non-Experts**: Designers must create interfaces that convey model uncertainty and explanations in ways that non-technical users can interpret without misrepresenting model capability (source: On the "Human" in Human-Artificial Intelligence Interaction.md).
