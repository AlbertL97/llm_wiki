## Main content

This source presents an overview of the scale of China's market presence, government funding, and corporate development in the embodied AI sector (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).

### 1. Market Dominance and Supply Chain
- **70% Global Market Share**: According to China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, China has produced nearly 100 different embodied AI robotic products and claims 70% of the global market share (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).
- **Stem-to-Stern Supply Chain**: This market leadership is driven by China's comprehensive supply chain for critical parts, combined with rapid progress in domestic AI models (such as DeepSeek) and specialized silicon chips (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).

### 2. State Funding and Employment Growth
- **$138 Billion Venture Fund**: The central Chinese government has allocated a $138 billion venture fund specifically targeted at the development of humanoid robots for both industrial complexes and personal consumer use (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).
- **Quadrupling Job Openings**: According to recruitment firm Galpin, job openings in China's humanoid robotics sector quadrupled year-over-year, leading to substantial salary increases for algorithm and robotics control engineers (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).

### 3. Unitree Consumer Humanoids and Pets
Hangzhou-based Unitree is highlighted as a consumer robotics pioneer (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md):
- **Unitree G1**: Sells on the web for $16,000. It is a 4'2" tall, 77 lb humanoid agent capable of running, jumping, and executing domestic chores like getting coffee (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).
- **Unitree Go2 Robot Dog**: Sells for $600. It is equipped with ultra-wide recognition lidar and has 12 flexible knee joint motors supporting complex balance and mobility maneuvers (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).

### 4. Lenovo Daystar Six-Legged Bot
- **Unsafe Environments**: Lenovo's six-legged Daystar inspection and diagnostic robot is deployed inside China's industrial facilities to patrol areas that are unreachable or unsafe for human workers (source: China's Next AI Breakthrough - Physical AI.md).
