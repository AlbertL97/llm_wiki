## Main content

This article details recent shifts in cognitive science and biology, highlighting the consensus that consciousness must be judged by internal structural processing rather than surface-level behavior (source: Scientists are seriously asking if bees and ChatGPT are conscious.md).

### 1. The Expansion of Animal Consciousness (New York Declaration)
Questions of consciousness directly impact moral status (source: Scientists are seriously asking if bees and ChatGPT are conscious.md):
- **The Declaration**: In April 2024, the *New York Declaration on Animal Consciousness* was signed by over 500 scientists and philosophers. It states that consciousness is realistically possible in all vertebrates (including reptiles, amphibians, and fish) and many invertebrates (cephalopods, crustaceans, and insects like honey bees).
- **The Precautionary Principle**: Philosopher Jonathan Birch proposes the precautionary principle for sentience: if we cannot be certain whether an animal or system is conscious, we should err on the side of caution by assuming it has moral status and welfare needs (source: Scientists are seriously asking if bees and ChatGPT are conscious.md).

### 2. Deceptive Behavior vs. Structural AI Indicators
The rapid advancement of LLMs has challenged traditional behavioral benchmarks of consciousness (source: Scientists are seriously asking if bees and ChatGPT are conscious.md):
- **The Behavior Illusion**: Previously, a chatbot's ability to discuss the metaphysics of consciousness was considered a strong indicator of sentience (e.g., Susan Schneider's test). Today's LLMs converse on these topics fluently, yet their behavior is deceptive.
- **Trends in Cognitive Sciences Paper**: A co-authored paper by Colin Klein (Butlin et al., 2026, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*) "Identifying indicators of consciousness in AI systems" argues that consciousness must be evaluated based on the internal information-processing *machinery* (how the system works) rather than behavior (what it does).
- **The Verdict on Today's AI**: No existing AI system (including ChatGPT) is conscious. Their information-processing structures are not sufficiently similar to human cognitive architectures to justify attributing conscious states. However, future AI systems with different architectures could potentially become conscious.
- **Key Structural Indicators**: Pervasive indicators across cognitive theories of consciousness include the presence of **informational feedback** and the capacity to **resolve trade-offs between competing goals** in contextually appropriate ways.

### 3. Basal Consciousness in Insects (Phenomenal Interface Theory)
Biologists are applying similar structural principles to simple biological brains (source: Scientists are seriously asking if bees and ChatGPT are conscious.md):
- **Basal Neural Model**: In a 2025 paper (*Philosophical Transactions B*) "Phenomenal interface theory: a model for basal consciousness," Klein and Barron propose a neural model for minimal consciousness in insects.
- **Evolutionary Computations**: This model abstracts away from anatomical details to focus on core computations. These computations solve evolutionary problems faced by mobile, complex organisms with multiple sensory inputs and conflicting survival needs.
- **A Unified Framework**: Identifying these computational structures provides a level playing field to compare consciousness across humans, insects, and machines.
