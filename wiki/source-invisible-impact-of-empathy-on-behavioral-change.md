# Invisible Impact of Empathy on Behavioral Change

This paper investigates the nuanced role of empathy in AI chatbots for physical activity (PA) coaching and its effect on behavioral change over a six-week period. The study developed three PA coaching chatbots on WhatsApp that varied only in their empathy levels. Participants in the study often struggled to differentiate between the empathy conditions, and the non-empathetic version was sometimes rated as more engaging and useful. However, the chatbots with higher empathy were associated with a greater overall increase in step counts and a faster improvement in the intention to follow advice. The findings suggest that empathy, while potentially difficult for users to explicitly identify, can still indirectly influence motivation, trust, and ultimately support sustained behavioral change, possibly through the peripheral route of the Elaboration Likelihood Model. The research emphasizes the importance of balancing chatbot effectiveness with human-like connection in design.

## Key Findings

*   Users found it difficult to distinguish between different levels of chatbot empathy.
*   The non-empathetic chatbot was sometimes rated as more engaging and useful.
*   Higher-empathy chatbots were linked to greater increases in step counts.
*   Higher-empathy chatbots showed faster improvement in participants' intention to follow advice.
*   Empathy's impact may be subtle and indirect, influencing motivation and trust rather than being explicitly recognized.
