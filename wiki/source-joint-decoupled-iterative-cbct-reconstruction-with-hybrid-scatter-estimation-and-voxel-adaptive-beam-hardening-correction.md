# Joint-decoupled iterative CBCT reconstruction with hybrid scatter estimation and voxel-adaptive beam hardening correction

## Main Findings

This paper introduces an advanced iterative framework for Cone-Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT) reconstruction designed to tackle significant artifacts caused by X-ray scatter and beam hardening. The proposed method innovatively decouples these two artifact types through a physics-driven optimization loop, allowing for more precise correction. It employs a hybrid strategy for scatter estimation, combining analytical methods with adaptive convolution, and utilizes a voxel-adaptive mechanism for beam hardening correction, directly refining image data without manual intervention. Comprehensive validation on biomedical phantoms demonstrates a substantial improvement in accuracy, significantly outperforming existing state-of-the-art techniques.

## Key Details

*   **Artifacts Addressed:** Scatter and beam hardening in CBCT.
*   **Methodology:** Joint-decoupled iterative reconstruction framework.
*   **Scatter Estimation:** Hybrid approach (analytical first-order, convolutional multiple scattering).
*   **Beam Hardening Correction:** Voxel-adaptive, iterative refinement.
*   **Validation:** Biomedical phantoms (Monte Carlo simulations and physical experiments).
*   **Results:** Marked reduction in mean relative error (e.g., 11.96% to 1.27% for head phantom).
