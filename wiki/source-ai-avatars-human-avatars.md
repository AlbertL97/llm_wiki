## Main content

### Overview
This study compares user interactions (via danmaku comments) with AI-Avatars (fully autonomous AI streamers, e.g., Neuro-sama) versus Human-Avatars (avatars operated by human teams) in live streaming environments (source: (1) (PDF) AI-Avatars Versus Human-Avatars_ A Comparative Study of User Interaction Behaviors in Live Streaming (1).md).

### Key Findings
1. **Motivation & Focus**: User engagement with AI-Avatars is primarily driven by **curiosity about AI**. Danmaku comments in AI-Avatar livestreams are diverse and information-seeking (testing the AI's capabilities).
2. **Human-Avatar Interactions**: Comments in Human-Avatar livestreams are more uniform, socially oriented, and focused on building interpersonal relationships.
3. **Ritual Chains & Social Intelligence**: While AI-Avatars demonstrate functional viability, their limited social intelligence prevents them from building **interaction ritual chains** (collective emotional energy buildup) and deep emotional resonance, limiting long-term user retention.
4. **CASA Paradigm**: The study validates the Computers Are Social Actors (CASA) paradigm, confirming that users apply social norms to autonomous AI agents, but shows a clear shift toward cognitive probing rather than standard socialization (source: (1) (PDF) AI-Avatars Versus Human-Avatars_ A Comparative Study of User Interaction Behaviors in Live Streaming (1).md).
