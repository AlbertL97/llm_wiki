# Public Perceptions of AI-Driven Decision-Making in Healthcare

This paper explores how the public perceives the use of AI in healthcare decision-making, focusing on helpfulness, risk, and fairness.

## Key Findings

*   **Perceived Helpfulness:** Increased familiarity with AI, confidence in clinicians' ability to discern AI content, and the use of conversational agents for health information were linked to higher perceptions of AI helpfulness in healthcare.
*   **Perceived Risk:** While conversational agents were associated with lower perceived risk, general AI familiarity and reliance on traditional health information sources were linked to higher perceived risk.
*   **Perceived Fairness:** Confidence in clinicians' oversight was the strongest predictor of perceived fairness, with smaller positive contributions from AI familiarity, AI literacy, and conversational agent use.
*   **Driver of Perceptions:** Ultimately, the study suggests that public trust in human healthcare professionals significantly outweighs trust in the AI technology itself when forming perceptions of AI's helpfulness and fairness in healthcare.
*   **Influencing Factors:** Public acceptance of AI in healthcare is shaped by technological familiarity, the use of specific AI tools like conversational agents, and the perceived competence of human oversight.

## Methodology

*   The study utilized Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) to analyze data from a large longitudinal survey panel (n=3,915).
*   Dependent variables included perceptions of AI as helpful, risky, and fair.
*   Exogenous variables encompassed AI literacy, AI familiarity, clinician confidence, and usage of conversational agents and traditional health information sources.
