# Assessing Physical Frailty and Fall-Risk Indicators with Social Robots: An in situ Evaluation with Older Adults

## Overview
This article details the development and evaluation of a social robot system designed to assist in the assessment of physical frailty and fall-risk indicators among older adults. The system aims to provide more objective and detailed biomechanical insights compared to traditional clinical assessments.

## Key Findings

*   **Novel Assessment Framework:** A robotic framework was developed to guide older adults through standardized frailty and fall-risk tests (SPPB, TUG) while capturing enhanced biomechanical data.
*   **Technological Integration:** The system utilizes a Behaviour Tree architecture and vision-based skeleton tracking for perception, decision-making, interaction, and measurement.
*   **In-Situ Evaluation:** The framework was evaluated over six months in a rehabilitation center with 81 older adults.
*   **High Reliability:** Robot-derived measurements demonstrated excellent agreement with therapist assessments and clinical reference instruments for crucial metrics like test completion times and gait parameters (ICC > 0.9).
*   **Robust Agreement:** Substantial agreement was found for overall SPPB scores between the robot and therapists (k=0.67), and moderate agreement with IMUs (k=0.55).
*   **Clinical Utility:** The study highlights the potential of social robots to offer reliable, objective frailty assessments and collect valuable mobility indicators in healthcare environments.
