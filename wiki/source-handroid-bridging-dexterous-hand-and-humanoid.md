# Handroid: Bridging Dexterous Hand and Humanoid

## Summary

The Handroid platform is a novel reconfigurable robot that can switch between a dexterous hand and a desktop humanoid embodiment. It is designed to facilitate research in morphology-reconfigurable robotics and cross-embodiment robot learning, offering a unified control framework for various manipulation and locomotion tasks.

## Key Findings

*   **Dual Embodiment:** Handroid is a single robot platform that can be reconfigured into two distinct embodiments: a dexterous anthropomorphic hand and a functional desktop humanoid robot.
*   **Integrated Capabilities:** This dual nature aims to combine the fine manipulation skills of dexterous hands with the mobility and whole-body interaction capabilities of humanoid robots.
*   **Technical Specifications:** The robot is desktop-scale (0.33 m height, 2.05 kg weight) and utilizes a 27-DoF electromechanical body. The hand embodiment dedicates 20 DoFs to an anthropomorphic hand, while the humanoid embodiment reconfigures these modules into a full body with locomotion capabilities (12-DoF lower-limb).
*   **Unified Control Framework:** A singular control and learning framework supports a wide range of tasks, including hand teleoperation, grasping, in-hand manipulation, humanoid locomotion, gait generation, and interactive motion authoring.
*   **Validation:** The platform's effectiveness is demonstrated through real-world experiments covering dexterous manipulation, reinforcement-learning-based locomotion, and complex tasks involving embodiment switching and pick-and-place operations.
*   **Research Contribution:** Handroid serves as a compact and reproducible platform for advancing research in morphology-reconfigurable robotics and cross-embodiment robot learning.
