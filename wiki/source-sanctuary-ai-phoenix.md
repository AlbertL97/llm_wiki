## Main content

This source presents Sanctuary AI's engineering focus on hand dexterity and tactile feedback in humanoid robotics (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).

### 1. Hands as the Work Interface
- **Manipulation Priority**: Unlike humanoid developers that prioritize bipedal walking dynamics, Sanctuary AI focuses on hand engineering and dexterous manipulation (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).
- **Physical Interface**: Human hands are the primary end effectors and the key interface to the physical world of work (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md). Hand capability is identified as the core bottleneck for humanoid commercial viability.

### 2. Tactile Touch Sensor Integration
- **Tactile Feedback**: Sanctuary AI equipped its 7th-generation Phoenix general-purpose humanoid with advanced touch sensors (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).
- **Vision + Touch Pairing**: Visual planning is paired with tactile feedback to execute complex work. Once hands approach objects, touch sensors provide rich data (textures, contact points) that are visually unavailable (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).
- **Blind Picking**: Tactile feedback is essential for "blind picking," where a robot must reach into a receptacle to retrieve components without direct line-of-sight visual feedback (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).

### 3. Force Correction and Operation Speed
- **Force Correction**: Tactile sensors enable the robot to measure the exact force exerted on objects, allowing real-time force correction during manipulation (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).
- **Task Confirmation**: Feeling the object allows the system to confirm that a physical task has been executed correctly, increasing operational speed, certainty, and confidence (source: Sanctuary AI adds tactile sensors to Phoenix humanoid robots.md).
