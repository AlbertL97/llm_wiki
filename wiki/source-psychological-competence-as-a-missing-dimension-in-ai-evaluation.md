# Psychological Competence as a Missing Dimension in AI Evaluation

## Introduction

This paper addresses a critical gap in current AI evaluation frameworks: the lack of focus on the psychological impact of AI systems on human users. While technical performance metrics (accuracy, robustness, etc.) are standard, they fail to capture the nuanced ways in which conversational AI influences user cognition, emotions, trust, and decision-making. As AI takes on roles as advisors, coaches, and companions, its interactions can profoundly shape user experience and outcomes.

## Psychological Competence: A New Evaluation Dimension

The authors introduce and define **psychological competence** as a necessary dimension for evaluating human-facing AI. This refers to an AI system's ability to:

*   Support user cognition.
*   Facilitate emotional interpretation.
*   Aid in behavioral decision-making.

Crucially, this support must be appropriate to the specific user, context, and purpose of the interaction.

## Key Components of Psychological Competence

Psychological competence is influenced by several interaction properties, including:

*   Framing of information
*   Tone of communication
*   Perceived authority of the AI
*   Responsiveness to user needs
*   Handling of uncertainty
*   Guidance provided during conversation

## Limitations of Existing Approaches

Existing evaluation methods often assess aspects of these properties but rarely measure the direct psychological effects on users comprehensively.

## Proposed Framework and Assessment

Drawing on behavioral science and human-AI interaction research, the paper outlines a conceptual framework for psychological competence and its core domains. It proposes methods for assessment, rather than a specific benchmark:

*   **Scenario-based probes:** Designing specific interaction scenarios to test AI responses.
*   **Structured human evaluation:** Employing human judges to assess psychological competence.
*   **Model-assisted evaluation:** Utilizing AI models to help evaluate psychological competence.

## Conclusion

The authors argue that psychological competence must become a fundamental consideration for AI model providers, deploying organizations, researchers, and regulators to ensure the responsible development and deployment of human-facing AI systems and to mitigate potential negative real-world effects.
