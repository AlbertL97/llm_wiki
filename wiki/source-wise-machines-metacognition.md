## Main content

This opinion article outlines the requirements for transitioning AI from analytical task optimization (smartness) to adaptive, robust reasoning in novel environments (wisdom) by structuring metacognitive layers into agent designs (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).

### 1. Wisdom vs. Smartness
- **Smartness**: AI's current capacity to perform well on specific, bounded analytical tasks using optimization or data processing (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
- **Wisdom**: A suite of capabilities designed to navigate **intractable problems**—those complex, real-world problems that lie completely outside the scope of analytical calculations or formal optimization techniques (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).

### 2. Object-Level vs. Metacognitive Strategies
Wisdom is structured as a two-tiered strategy framework (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md):
- **Object-Level Strategies**: Direct methods used to generate potential solutions, including *heuristics* (using data subsets to manage problems) and *narrative thinking* (constructing analogical and causal models to evaluate choices) (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
- **Metacognitive Strategies**: Strategies used to manage the fit of object-level strategies to the current task (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md). Examples include *intellectual humility* (recognizing model limits), *perspective-taking* (perspectival metacognition to integrate viewpoints), and *context adaptability* (generalizing to novel environments) (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).

### 3. Core Metacognitive Processes
To implement wise metacognition, agent architectures must run three types of parallel processes (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md):
1. **Input-Seeking Process**: Metacognitively identifying and seeking the missing inputs/data required for object-level heuristics to execute successfully (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
2. **Conflict Resolution Process**: Evaluating and selecting the best strategy when multiple object-level heuristics or narrative models conflict (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
3. **Outcome-Monitoring Process**: Performing "sanity checks" to verify if the outputs of the selected object-level strategy are plausible in the real world (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).

### 4. Benefits of Wise AI Metacognition
Integrating these metacognitive structures results in significant safety and performance benefits (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md):
- **Robustness**: Makes the agent highly reliable, flexible, and capable of generalizing to novel environments (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
- **Explainability**: Allows the agent to communicate its decision-making process and underlying reasoning to human users (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
- **Cooperative AI**: Enables agents to work with other AIs or humans toward shared goals using social understanding and credible commitments (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
- **Safety**: Minimizes the risk of both prosaic errors and catastrophic goal misalignment by checking outcomes and recognizing limitations (source: Imagining and building wise machines_ the centrality of AI metacognition.md).
