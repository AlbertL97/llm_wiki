# Source Summary: Co-Creativity at the Table: A Qualitative Analysis of Creative Interactions in the Podcast "Adventure AI"

## Overview
This paper presents a qualitative analysis of human-AI interactions observed in the "Adventure AI" podcast, which chronicles Dungeons & Dragons gameplay involving both human and artificial intelligence participants. The study focuses on understanding how AI is integrated into collaborative creative processes, player perceptions of AI, and the nature of their interactions.

## Key Findings
*   **Roles of AI and Humans:** The research identifies distinct and evolving roles for both AI and human players in the co-creative environment of tabletop role-playing games.
*   **AI Performance Evaluation:** Players engage in evaluating the AI's contributions, noting both its successes in complex narrative and mechanical aspects, as well as its limitations and failures.
*   **Anthropomorphic Treatment:** A significant theme is the extent to which human players treat the AI as a 'person' or 'character' at the table, indicating aspects of anthropomorphism and social cognition towards the AI.
*   **Appropriate AI Usage:** The analysis provides insights into scenarios where AI is most effective and where it is less suitable within creative gaming contexts, offering guidance for future AI design in such applications.

## Method
The study employed a qualitative analysis approach, examining three seasons (2023-2025) of the "Adventure AI" podcast to gather data on human-AI interactions and perceptions in a Dungeons & Dragons setting.
