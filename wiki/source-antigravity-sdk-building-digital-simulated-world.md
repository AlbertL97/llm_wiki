# Antigravity SDK: Building a digital simulated world

## Introduction
This article details the Google Antigravity SDK, a development kit used to construct digital simulated environments. Specifically, it highlights an application built for Google I/O: a multi-agent social simulation featuring virtual avatars of attendees interacting autonomously within a simulated space station.

## Key Findings
*   **Tool for Social Simulation:** The Antigravity SDK provides a framework for creating complex digital simulated worlds, enabling the deployment and observation of multi-agent systems.
*   **Autonomous Agent Interaction:** The demonstrated application involved virtual avatars of real attendees that operated autonomously, engaging in social interactions within the simulated environment without direct human control.
*   **Platform for Studying Social Dynamics:** Such simulations serve as a valuable platform for researchers to study emergent social behaviors, interaction patterns, and agent-to-agent dynamics in a controlled digital setting.
*   **Potential for Human-Agent Research:** While focused on agent-agent interaction, the underlying technology has implications for understanding how humans might perceive and interact with autonomous agents and simulated social worlds.
