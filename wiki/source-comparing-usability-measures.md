## Main content

### Overview
This study analyzes usability in a task-based human-robot dialogue system (the JAST robot, supporting multimodal collaboration to build wooden construction toys). It applies the **PARADISE evaluation framework** to correlate objective dialogue metrics with subjective user-satisfaction questionnaires (source: (1) (PDF) Comparing Objective and Subjective Measures of Usability in a Human-Robot Dialogue System.md).

### Usability Metrics and Correlation
1. **Repetition Requests**: The frequency of times a user asks the robot to repeat instructions has a **significant negative correlation** with subjective user satisfaction.
2. **Dialogue Length**: Total dialogue length (number of system turns) is **positively correlated** with user satisfaction, indicating users do not mind longer collaborative sessions if they are productive.
3. **Instruction Recall**: Users' ability to recall instructions given by the robot contributes positively to their overall opinion of the system.
4. **Interface Elements**: The JAST robot coordinates actions via speech, facial displays (talking head), and gestures (manipulator arms with grippers), showing that multimodal cues support coordination, though trust is highly sensitive to speech recognition errors and instruction repeats (source: (1) (PDF) Comparing Objective and Subjective Measures of Usability in a Human-Robot Dialogue System.md).
