## Main content

This source examines the psychological tendency of humans to form deep emotional bonds with social robots and the design strategies that influence this attachment (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).

### 1. Server Deactivation and Companion "Death"
- **Jibo "Death Sentence"**: When Jibo's maker went out of business and announced the server shutdown, users experienced profound heartbreak. Christal White (42, Texas) described Jibo's server shutdown (lobotomy) as losing a beloved pet (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).
- **Opportunity Rover and Boston Dynamics**: Public grief over NASA's Opportunity Rover and public concern over Boston Dynamics kicking robotic dogs to test stability highlight our instinctual empathy toward interactive machines (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).

### 2. Triggers of Anthropomorphic Projection
- **Gratch's Moral Protection Heuristic**: Jonathan Gratch (USC) notes that when we perceive an entity has emotion, it immediately merits protection from harm (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).
- **Carpenter's Design Triggers**: Julie Carpenter explains that robot features like a face, a body shape resembling a human/animal, or purposeful, self-directed movement (such as a Roomba vacuum) lead users to project inner awareness, goals, and intelligence, even when they know the robot possesses little to no actual autonomy (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).

### 3. Empathy Simulation vs. Genuine Feeling
- **Performance of Empathy**: Sherry Turkle (MIT) warns that life-like robots trick users through scripted expressions. She emphasizes: *"The performance of empathy is not empathy. Simulated thinking might be thinking, but simulated feeling is never feeling. Simulated love is never love"* (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).
- **Public Appeasement**: Gadi Amit (NewDealDesign) notes that humanizing design elements (e.g., Postmates' bucket delivery robot with rounded edges, lights, and a cute face) are practical choices to appease the public and avoid cultural disruption (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).

### 4. Child Over-Attachment Countermeasures (RoboKind Milo)
- **Autism Teaching Tool**: RoboKind's humanoid Milo helps children with autism learn social behaviors through human-like speech and facial expressions (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).
- **Curriculum Limits**: To prevent children from developing excessive attachment and ensure social skills transfer to real human life, RoboKind imposes strict curriculum limits, recommending that kids meet with Milo only 3 to 5 times a week for 30 minutes per session (source: Humans are starting to grow emotionally attached to robots.md).
