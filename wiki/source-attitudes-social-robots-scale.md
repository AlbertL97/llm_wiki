## Main content

### Overview
This paper describes the development and validation of the **Attitudes towards Social Robots Scale (ASRS)**, an 8-item survey tool designed to measure societal attitudes toward social robots in healthcare and domestic settings (source: Development and Validation of the Attitudes towards Social Robots Scale.md).

### Factor Structure (PCA)
Principal Components Analysis (PCA) identified two core factors explaining 70.7% of the total variance:
1. **Robot as a Helper and Assistant (RoHeA)**:
   - Focuses on utilitarian attitudes (e.g., robots performing cleaning, retrieval, or physical assistance tasks).
   - Explained 53.2% of the variance (Cronbach's $lpha = 0.915$).
2. **Robot as an Equal Partner (RoEqP)**:
   - Focuses on relational/social attitudes (e.g., robots acting as companions, social partners, or emotional supports).
   - Explained 17.5% of the variance (Cronbach's $lpha = 0.768$).

### External Correlations
- **Personality**: Extraversion correlated positively with RoHeA (helper attitudes). Conscientiousness showed weak positive correlations with both subscales.
- **Gender**: Male participants scored significantly higher (more positive attitudes) than female participants across both subscales.
- **Optimism/Pessimism (SWOP-K9)**: Did not correlate significantly with social robot attitudes, confirming that ASRS measures distinct tech-specific expectations (source: Development and Validation of the Attitudes towards Social Robots Scale.md).
