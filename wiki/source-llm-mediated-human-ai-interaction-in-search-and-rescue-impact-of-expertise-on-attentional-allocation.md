# LLM-Mediated Human-AI Interaction in Search and Rescue: Impact of Expertise on Attentional Allocation

## Introduction
Human-AI teaming (HAT) is becoming increasingly prevalent, with AI systems offering real-time, context-aware guidance in complex tasks like search and rescue (SAR). The effectiveness of these systems hinges on their impact on human cognition and behavior, potentially influencing attention, planning, and interaction with the task environment. Crucially, these effects can differ based on the user's level of expertise.

## Methodology
This study investigated the mechanisms of LLM-mediated interaction in a simulated SAR environment. The research compared human performance under two conditions with LLM guidance against a baseline condition without an LLM. The analysis incorporated multiple levels of interaction, including task performance metrics, eye-tracking data to understand attention allocation and AI interaction, and behavioral measures to assess decision-making patterns over time.

## Key Findings

*   **Improved Task Efficiency:** LLM guidance significantly enhanced task efficiency, demonstrated by higher rewards and a greater number of victims found per step.
*   **Limitations in Overall Success:** Despite efficiency improvements, the LLM guidance did not result in an increase in the total number of victims saved.
*   **Attention Dynamics:** Eye-tracking revealed an "attention-guidance trade-off." Participants allocated more visual attention to the chat interface, and exhibited increased pupil size variability, indicating cognitive shifts or load.
*   **Expertise as a Moderator:** The impact of LLM guidance was moderated by user expertise:
    *   **Novice Users:** Showed a tendency towards passive reliance on the AI's guidance.
    *   **Expert Users:** Demonstrated a more active approach, maintaining a "verification loop" by consistently scanning the environment and cross-referencing AI recommendations with their own observations.

## Conclusion
The findings suggest that the efficacy of LLM-mediated human-AI teaming is contingent upon the operator's capacity to critically evaluate and cross-reference AI-provided guidance with real-world, or ground truth, information. This verification process is essential for maintaining situational awareness, particularly when relying on AI for complex and critical tasks.
