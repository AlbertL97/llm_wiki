## Main content

### Overview
This source covers a ResearchGate academic discussion initiated by Alessandro Rugolo on March 8, 2026. The discussion addresses the critical gap in human-AI interaction literature regarding the trajectory of human trust when expectations suddenly break down during real-world AI failures (source: (1) What happens when trust in an AI system breaks_ Do human-AI relationships follow recognizable trajectories_.md).

### Core Discussion Points
1. **Trust Calibration vs. Breakdown**: While much research focuses on static trust calibration and explainability, real interactions involve dynamic moments where expectations shatter due to unexpected model actions or errors.
2. **Expectation Realignment**: The trajectory of human-AI relationships following a breakdown depends on how the model communicates uncertainty, handles error recovery, and adapts to user feedback.
3. **Human-like Attitude Risks**: The discussion addresses whether AI systems adopting human-like personas and apologies (empathy performance) risk misleading users about actual machine capabilities, potentially worsening the trust collapse when subsequent errors occur (source: (1) What happens when trust in an AI system breaks_ Do human-AI relationships follow recognizable trajectories_.md).
