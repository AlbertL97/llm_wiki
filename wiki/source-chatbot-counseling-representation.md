## Main content

### Overview
This study ($N=385$ US adults) investigates the effect of chatbot representation (human-like vs. machine-like) on user compliance with mental health recommendations (source: Human vs. machine-like representation in chatbot mental health counseling_ the serial mediation of psychological distance and trust on compliance intention.md).

### Core Findings
1. **Higher Compliance**: Participants interacting with a chatbot featuring a human-like representation (name, human avatar, warm conversational style) showed a significantly higher intention to comply with counseling recommendations than those in the machine-like condition.
2. **Serial Mediation Model**:
   - The effect of representation on compliance is serially mediated: **Human representation $ightarrow$ Reduced Psychological Distance $ightarrow$ Increased Perceived Trust $ightarrow$ Higher Compliance Intention**.
   - Reducing psychological distance (perceived intimacy and closeness) is a necessary precursor for trust formation in virtual counseling.
3. **Implied Design Trade-offs**: While human representation boosts trust and compliance, it risks encouraging inappropriate over-reliance or user disappointment if the chatbot fails to provide actual empathetic depth (source: Human vs. machine-like representation in chatbot mental health counseling_ the serial mediation of psychological distance and trust on compliance intention.md).
