# A Psychotechnology in the Making: Why Conversational AI Reorganizes Thinking Regardless of AGI

## Key Findings

*   Conversational AI, distinct from its intelligence, is developing into a "psychotechnology" because its conversational medium reshapes human thinking.
*   The medium of conversational AI is format-identical to self-directed thought, presented as addressed, contingent language.
*   The paper proposes four criteria for a practice to be a psychotechnology: repetition, redistribution of cognitive labor, social transmission/institutional stabilization, and effects outlasting single episodes.
*   Web search is presented as a critical comparator, already acting as a psychotechnology for retrieval and transactive memory.
*   Conversational AI's unique contribution as a psychotechnology is its medium, which can accelerate trust, make repair feel like consultation, blur source monitoring, and transform defaults into roles.
*   Evidence suggests immediate influence, redistributed work during use, and some bounded persistence of effects.
*   The paper distinguishes between AI's medium as a psychotechnology in the making and specific conversational capabilities.
