## Main content

### Overview
This laboratory experiment investigates how user personality traits (propensity to trust automation, negative attitudes toward robots) and emotional states (state anxiety) influence trust and physical comfort distance during initial encounters with a humanoid domestic robot (source: More Than a Feeling-Interrelation of Trust Layers in Human-Robot Interaction and the Role of User Dispositions and State Anxiety.md).

### Interdependent Trust Layers
The study supports a three-layer model of trust in HRI:
1. **Dispositional Trust**: General propensity to trust technology/automation.
2. **Initial Learned Trust**: Trust formed at first sight based on appearance and initial framing.
3. **Dynamic Learned Trust**: Trust continuously updated through actual interaction.

### Psychological Mechanisms
- **State Anxiety Mediation**: Propensity to trust automation does not directly determine initial learned trust; instead, it is mediated by **state anxiety**. Low propensity to trust automation increases state anxiety during the robot's approach, which in turn reduces initial learned trust.
- **Negative Attitudes**: A pre-existing negative attitude toward robots directly decreases initial learned trust.
- **Comfort Distance**: Initial learned trust is a strong predictor of the physical distance at which users feel comfortable allowing a robot to approach, demonstrating that subjective trust directly influences spatial HRI behaviors (source: More Than a Feeling-Interrelation of Trust Layers in Human-Robot Interaction and the Role of User Dispositions and State Anxiety.md).
