## Main content

This source presents an overview of collaborative research initiatives examining human-robot cooperation and augmentation through a neuroergonomic lens (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).

### 1. The LEARNER Framework for Emergency Response
- **Augmentation Technology**: The LEARNER project (Learning Environments with Advanced Robotics for Next-generation Emergency Responders) integrates collaborative semi-autonomous ground robots, powered exoskeletons, and augmented reality (AR) to assist emergency responders (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).
- **Cognitive & Physical Augmentations**: Exoskeletons augment physical capacity while preserving responder autonomy, whereas AR serves as a cognitive scaffold to support wayfinding, situational collaboration, decision-making, and robot operation (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).

### 2. The AMELIA Work 4.0 Architecture
- **Cognitive Augmentation**: The AMELIA project (AugMEnted Learning InnovAtion) aims to augment human cognition for safer collaborative robot interaction (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).
- **HRI Capability Models**: AMELIA establishes neurophysiological, cognitive, and socio-behavioral capability models (workload, cognitive load, fatigue, stress, affect, and trust) during HRI—referred to as the "mind motor machine nexus" (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).
- **Failure Classification & AR Mutual Learning**: The project classifies HRI scenarios vulnerable to observable system failures. It uses physiological/cognitive models to dynamically adjust the user's state via an AR layer that allows humans and robots to mutually learn and communicate their states (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).

### 3. Disaster Response HRI
- **Operator Physiological Metrics**: Researchers systematically explore how disaster recovery environments affect HRI. They collect physiological and cognitive data (e.g., during Hurricanes Harvey/Michael and the Kilauea Volcano Eruption) to analyze operator states under extreme fatigue, stress, and insufficient training (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).

### 4. Human-Exoskeleton Interactions
- **Joint Load Reduction**: Exoskeleton cooperation (passive assistance to powered augmentation) reduces injury risks by transferring biomechanical loading away from targeted joints (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).
- **Neuroergonomic Fit**: Research addresses the "motor-mind-machine" mismatch during fatiguing occupation tasks by assessing exoskeleton learnability, usability, and safety in real-world workplaces (source: Human Robot Interactions.md).
