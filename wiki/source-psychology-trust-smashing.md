## Main content

### Overview
This Smashing Magazine guide outlines the design principles, measurement frameworks, and ethical responsibilities of building trust in generative and agentic AI systems (source: The Psychology Of Trust In AI_ A Guide To Measuring And Designing For User Confidence.md).

### The Four Pillars of AI Trust
Trust in AI is built upon four psychological dimensions:
1. **Ability (Competence)**: Does the system consistently perform tasks accurately?
2. **Benevolence**: Does the system act in the user's best interest?
3. **Integrity**: Does the system follow ethical rules and maintain transparency?
4. **Predictability & Reliability**: Does the system behave consistently and predictably?

### Measuring Trust
UX researchers should measure trust across three categories:
- **Qualitative Probes**: Interviewing users for trust-related language and checking for existential fears.
- **Quantitative Scales**: Utilizing academic scales (like the MDMT - Multidimensional Trust Scale).
- **Behavioral Metrics**: Observing actual user actions (e.g., rate of checking the AI's work, overriding actions).

### Design and Trust Repair
- **Explainability**: Provide context-appropriate transparency without 'trustwashing' (misrepresenting capability).
- **Graceful Error Handling**: Clearly state when the AI doesn't know an answer or makes a mistake, immediately offering steps for trust repair (source: The Psychology Of Trust In AI_ A Guide To Measuring And Designing For User Confidence.md).
