# Evaluating RE Practices for Explainability: Synthesizing Insights from Daimler Truck into an Explainable RE Framework Proposal

**Last updated:** 2026-07-14

## Introduction
Explainability is a crucial requirement for AI systems, particularly in safety-critical and regulated sectors. Despite existing research on explainability frameworks and user-centered methods, there is limited empirical understanding of how current Requirements Engineering (RE) practices support explainability requirements throughout the RE lifecycle, especially within industrial contexts.

## Study Details
This paper reports early findings from an ongoing industry-based study at Daimler Truck that investigates how explainability requirements are elicited, specified, and validated using established RE techniques. A multi-phase qualitative study was conducted with eight practitioners, employing think-aloud protocols and moderated group discussions across the elicitation, specification, and validation steps.

## Key Findings

*   **Challenges in RE Practices:** The study identified recurring challenges across all stages of the RE process concerning explainability requirements:
    *   **Elicitation:** Conceptual ambiguity was frequently encountered.
    *   **Specification:** Limitations in testability and expressiveness of requirements were observed.
    *   **Validation:** Validation efforts were fragmented due to vague criteria and regulatory uncertainty.
*   **Inadequate Support:** Current RE practices provide limited systematic support for addressing explainability requirements.

## Contribution & Future Work

The research contributes empirical insights into step-specific and cross-cutting challenges in RE for explainable AI. The paper outlines a research vision aimed at developing an empirically grounded RE framework for explainable AI-based systems.
