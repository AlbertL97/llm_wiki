## Main content

### Overview
This narrative review maps the 30-year longitudinal shift from **trust in automation** (focused on mechanical reliability) to **trust in AI** (focused on complex decision-making and opacity) in clinical healthcare (source: From Trust in Automation to Trust in AI in Healthcare_ A 30-Year Longitudinal Review and an Interdisciplinary Framework.md).

### The I-HATR Framework
The **Interdisciplinary Human-AI Trust Research (I-HATR)** framework bridges the gap between Explainable AI (XAI) and Human-Computer Interaction/Human Factors Engineering (HCI/HFE):
1. **XAI Pathway (Model-centric)**: Focuses on model transparency, moving from black-box models to ante-hoc (inherently interpretable) or post-hoc (LIME, SHAP) explanations.
2. **HCI/HFE Pathway (User-centric)**: Focuses on usability, cognitive load, alert burden, and situational awareness.
3. **Trust Determinants in Healthcare**:
   - **User Characteristics**: Clinician experience, general propensity to trust, state anxiety.
   - **AI System Attributes**: Performance, transparency, uncertainty communication.
   - **Contextual Factors**: Clinical workload, organizational policies, severity of patient condition.
4. **Measurement Evolution**: Traces the transition from static post-task self-reports to real-time, dynamic, multimodal assessments using behavioral tracking and physiological sensors (EEG, eye-tracking) (source: From Trust in Automation to Trust in AI in Healthcare_ A 30-Year Longitudinal Review and an Interdisciplinary Framework.md).
