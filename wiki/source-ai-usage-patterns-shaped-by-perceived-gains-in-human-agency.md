# AI usage patterns are shaped by perceived gains in human agency

## Findings

This research explores the drivers behind sustained engagement with conversational AI systems, suggesting that perceived gains in human agency are a more potent influence than traditional metrics like accuracy or reliability. The study, utilizing ethnographic data from daily AI chatbot users, found that individuals continue to use these tools because they feel more empowered, even if the AI's performance is inconsistent.

*   **Agency Over Trust:** User engagement with AI is strongly correlated with the feeling of enhanced individual agency. This psychological benefit often supersedes concerns about the AI's trustworthiness or accuracy.
*   **Limitations of Current Models:** Existing frameworks for understanding human-AI interaction, particularly those centered on trust, are insufficient to capture the nuances of long-term AI usage patterns.
*   **Sociotechnical Context:** AI usage is analyzed as a situated practice, embedded within the social and technical contexts of users' lives.
*   **The Agency Paradox:** A critical observation is the potential disconnect between immediate feelings of empowerment and actual, long-term structural or material empowerment.
*   **Future Directions:** The paper calls for the development of new behavioral frameworks and AI benchmarks that prioritize and effectively foster substantial, sustained human agency.
