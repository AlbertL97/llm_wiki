# Survey of Integrating Localized Speech Recognition Models in Robotic Systems

## Introduction
This survey provides an overview of integrating Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) models within robotic systems, particularly focusing on localized ASR solutions as an alternative or complement to cloud-based API services. ASR is identified as a key technology for enabling natural human-robot interaction (HRI).

## Key Findings and Discussion Points
*   **Importance of ASR in HRI:** The paper emphasizes that ASR is a critical component for intuitive and natural interaction between humans and robots.
*   **Beyond Cloud APIs:** It questions the sole reliance on online API services for ASR and explores the benefits and feasibility of localized ASR models.
*   **ASR Technology Overview:** The survey covers the evolution of ASR, from traditional methods to advanced deep learning models like OpenAI's Whisper, and highlights relevant datasets and toolkits.
*   **Robotics Integration:** A significant portion of the paper is dedicated to how ASR models are deployed in various intelligent robots and machines. This includes an analysis of different ASR model families and deployment strategies such as ROS-based, cloud-based, and hybrid approaches.
*   **Real-World Applications:** The document references real-world robotic platforms that utilize these ASR integrations.
*   **Challenges and Future Trends:** The authors identify the current challenges in implementing robust ASR in robots and discuss future research directions, including the potential of multimodal interaction for enhanced HRI.

## Conclusion
The paper serves as a valuable resource for social robotics researchers, offering insights into the practical aspects of integrating ASR for improved language-based human-robot interaction.
