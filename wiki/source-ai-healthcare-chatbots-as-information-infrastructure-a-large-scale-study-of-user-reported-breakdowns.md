# Source Summary: AI Healthcare Chatbots as Information Infrastructure: A Large-Scale Study of User-Reported Breakdowns

This study examines user-reported breakdowns in AI healthcare chatbots by analyzing over 15,000 reviews from 59 applications. It identifies three primary categories of issues: access barriers and service unreliability, poor user experience and interaction quality, and problems with billing and customer support. Notably, privacy and security concerns were linked to the most negative user experiences. By viewing these chatbots as information infrastructures, the research underscores the importance of usability, reliability, and trust in digital health systems.

## Key Findings

*   **Breakdown Categories:**
    *   Access barriers and service unreliability.
    *   User experience and interaction quality.
    *   Billing and customer support issues.
*   **Most Negative Experiences:** Privacy and security concerns significantly contributed to negative user outcomes.
*   **Framing:** AI healthcare chatbots are conceptualized as critical information infrastructures.
*   **Impact:** Failures in these areas, particularly trust-related aspects, negatively affect users.
