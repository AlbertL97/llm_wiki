# The complexities of patient-centred conversational artificial intelligence

Last updated: 2026-07-11

## Main Findings

*   Consumer-facing health chatbots using LLMs for symptom assessment often rely on idealized, simulated patients for development and evaluation.
*   Analysis of 2,053 real patient-chatbot conversations revealed significant variation in communication patterns and emotional expression among users.
*   A patient simulator was created, modeling clinical content, emotional state, conversational strategy, and communication style.
*   In a Turing-inspired evaluation, human graders could barely distinguish simulated from real conversations (55% accuracy).
*   When evaluating four LLMs for urgency assessment across 1,164 cases and five patient personae, communication style was found to significantly impact triage outcomes.
*   Patient-centered conversational AI needs to account for communication diversity; systems based on idealized interactions may underperform and worsen health disparities.
