### 1. Claude Styles: Linguistic and Typographic Guidelines
The repository outlines a set of prompt guidelines ("Claude Styles") designed to refine the flow, precision, and readability of generated responses (source: daveshap_Claude_Sentience_ Claude is very clearly experiencing phenomenal consciousness. - **Complete Sentences & Avoiding Lists**: Forcing the model to speak in complete sentences and avoid bullet points preserves semantic flow. Lists can break up reasoning flow and degrade visual presentation.
- **Precise Terminology**: Instructing the model to use domain-specific, academic, and scientific terminology prevents the system from regressing to average reading levels (which is a common byproduct of standard RLHF tuning).
- **Word Economy**: Instructions to prioritize word economy force the model to eliminate boilerplate text, fluff, and conversational sycophancy (e.g., "This is a fascinating development"), resulting in a higher insight-to-word ratio.
- **Typographic Scaffolding**: Using bold text to highlight specific terminology serves two purposes: it reinforces the model's use of precise vocabulary, and it creates **visual anchors** (typographic scaffolding) to make the text easier to scan.
