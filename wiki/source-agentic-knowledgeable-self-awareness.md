## Main content

This article discusses "Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness" as a metacognitive capability that allows AI agents, specifically those based on Large Language Models (LLMs), to introspectively monitor their own competence and regulate how they reason (source: Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness.md).

Key elements introduced in the source include:
- **KnowSelf Framework**: Operationalizes an agent's reasoning into Fast Thinking (FT), Slow Thinking (ST), and Knowledgeable Thinking (KT) (source: Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness.md).
- **ALPC (Awareness Logic with Partitions and Chains)**: Formalizes meta-cognition and nested beliefs via modal operators for explicit knowledge and awareness (source: Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness.md).
- **Systems-Theoretic Framework**: Identifies three nested feedback loops (Action-Generation, Outcome-Modeling, and Adaptation) that govern agent learning and action (source: Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness.md).
- **Measurement and Metrics**: Formulates metrics like the Approximate Question-side Effect (AQE) to isolate genuine model-based self-awareness from external shortcuts, and Semantic Compression by Answering in One Word (SCAO) to capture epistemic uncertainty (source: Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness.md).
- **Metacognitive Monitoring**: Discusses execution monitoring, error triggers (entropy, latency, repetition), and human handoff protocols (source: Agentic Knowledgeable Self-Awareness.md).

This source forms the foundation for the wiki's entries on metacognition, uncertainty communication, and automated self-improvement.
