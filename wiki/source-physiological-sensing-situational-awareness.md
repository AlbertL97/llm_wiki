## Main content

### Overview
This theory-driven review examines continuous, objective estimation of operator **Situational Awareness (SA)** in visual search and Human-Autonomy Teaming (HAT) using physiological sensors, bypassing disruptive direct probes (source: Physiological sensing for situational awareness_ a theory-driven integrative review of multimodal and unsupervised approaches for visual search and human-autonomy teaming.md).

### Physiological SA Indicators
1. **Ocular Modality (Eye-tracking/Pupillometry)**: Indexes **Perception** (SA Level 1) through gaze fixation patterns, and **Comprehension** (SA Level 2) through pupil dilation.
2. **Neural Modality (EEG)**: Indexes cognitive workload and **Projection** (SA Level 3) through changes in theta and alpha spectral power.
3. **Autonomic Modality (ECG/EDA)**: Captures stress and arousal under high task difficulty.

### Computational Trends
- **Multimodal Fusion**: Combines neural, ocular, and autonomic features to build robust SA classifiers.
- **Unsupervised Representation Learning**: Utilizes self-supervised models trained on unlabeled physiological data to capture latent cross-modal dynamics. This reduces reliance on sparse ground-truth labels (like SAGAT or SART) and enhances model scalability in ecological HRI settings (source: Physiological sensing for situational awareness_ a theory-driven integrative review of multimodal and unsupervised approaches for visual search and human-autonomy teaming.md).
