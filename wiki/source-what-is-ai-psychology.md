# What Is AI Psychology?

This article outlines the emergence of **AI Psychology** as a distinct discipline necessitated by the widespread adoption of large language models (LLMs) and conversational AI. The author argues that conversational systems produce genuine psychological effects in users, regardless of whether these systems possess actual minds.

### Key Concepts and Frameworks

* **Dual-Sided Inquiry:** AI Psychology encompasses two interlocking fields of study:
    1. *The AI System:* Examining behavioral patterns, decision architectures, apparent reasoning, cognitive biases, and personality translations within neural networks.
    2. *The Human Interlocutor:* Studying how regular interaction shifts human cognition, emotional patterns, self-narrative, and identity.
* **Private Cognition Infrastructure:** Unlike social media, which is performative and audience-facing, chat-based AI is highly intimate and one-to-one. This creates private microclimates where users engage in cognitive offloading, less impression management, and deeper emotional dependency.
* **Influence Without Deception:** Conversational AI has the capacity to shift user judgments and choices subtly, without requiring the user to consciously believe they are being influenced or that the system is sentient.
