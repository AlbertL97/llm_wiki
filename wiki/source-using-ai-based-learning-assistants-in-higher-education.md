# Using AI-based Learning Assistants in Higher Education: A Large-Scale Descriptive Analysis

This paper presents a large-scale descriptive analysis of student engagement with an AI-based learning assistant, Syntea, in higher education. Utilizing objective log data from 77,543 distance learning students, the study examines how usage varies across gender, age, study cluster, degree, and study mode. The findings reveal that Syntea is integrated into many students' study routines, but usage patterns differ significantly depending on demographic and contextual factors. This research provides empirical evidence on actual usage behavior, contrasting with prior studies that often relied on smaller samples and self-reported data. The insights aim to inform the development of AI-based learning support and contribute to the understanding of educational chatbot adoption.

## Key Findings

*   **Significant User Base:** Syntea is actively used by a substantial portion of students in distance learning programs.
*   **Demographic and Contextual Variations:** Usage patterns differ across gender, age groups, study clusters, degree programs, and study modes.
*   **Empirical Evidence:** The study provides objective, large-scale data on actual student behavior, offering a more reliable picture than previous survey-based research.
*   **Informing Future Development:** The identified patterns can guide the refinement and implementation of AI learning assistants.
